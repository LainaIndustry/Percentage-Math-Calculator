How Discount Calculation Works

Discount Formula

The discount calculation uses this formula:

Discount Amount = Original Price √ó (Discount Percentage √∑ 100)

Final Price = Original Price - Discount Amount

Step-by-Step Example

If an item costs $100 and has a 25% discount:

  1. Calculate discount amount: $100 √ó (25 √∑ 100) = $25
  2. Subtract discount from original price: $100 - $25 = $75
  3. Final price after discount: $75
  4. You save: $25

Discount Calculator FAQs

Use this formula: Discount % = [(Original Price - Sale Price) √∑ Original Price] √ó 100

Example: Original $100, Sale $75

[(100 - 75) √∑ 100] √ó 100 = 25% discount

Percentage off: Discount is calculated as a percentage of the original price (e.g., 20% off $100 = $20 off).

Fixed amount off: A specific dollar amount is subtracted (e.g., $20 off $100 = 20% discount if price is $100).

Apply discounts sequentially. For example, 20% off then 10% off:

1. First discount: $100 - 20% = $80

2. Second discount: $80 - 10% = $72

Total discount is not 30% but 28% (from $100 to $72).

This calculator shows the item price discount only. For total cost including tax and shipping:

1. Calculate item discount first

2. Add tax percentage to discounted price

3. Add fixed shipping cost

Tax and shipping are usually applied after discount.
